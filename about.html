About AKANA

"Rooted in Heritage. Driven by Care. United for a Healthier Tomorrow."

The Association of Kenyan American Nurse Anesthetists (AKANA) is a professional network uniting Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetists of Kenyan origin and heritage in the United States. Our mission is to promote unity, professional growth, and collaboration among Kenyan-American nurse anesthetists, while advocating for excellence in anesthesia care and education.

Our Mission

To empower Kenyan American nurse anesthetists through community, education, and mentorship, and to contribute to advancements in safe anesthesia practice both in the U.S. and Kenya.

Our Vision

A global community of nurse anesthetists connected by heritage and excellence, working together to improve surgical and anesthesia outcomes worldwide.

Our Story

AKANA was founded in 2020 by a group of Kenyan-American CRNAs who recognized the need for a dedicated platform to support their unique community. Starting with just a handful of members, the association has grown to hundreds of practitioners and students across the United States. We take pride in our roots – drawing strength from Kenya’s rich heritage – and our shared goal of improving patient care through professional development.

Over the years, AKANA has launched mentorship programs pairing seasoned CRNAs with students (SRNAs), organized annual conferences and virtual grand rounds, and raised funds to support anesthesia education in under-resourced Kenyan hospitals. Each milestone reinforces our commitment to being not just an organization, but a family bound by purpose and pride.
